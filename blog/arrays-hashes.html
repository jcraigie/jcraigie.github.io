Arrays and Hashes

1/24/2016

Arrays are a way of storing information without having to assign each piece of information its own name. Instead an array stores that information as a list with each piece of information having its own place that is also referenced by a number. Hashs are similar to a list in that they store info but instead of having a predetermined number assigned to the stored value the user can define the key as they wish much like a dictionary uses a word to refer to its definition. Arrays and hashs may seem similar, and they are to an extent, but they each have their place.
